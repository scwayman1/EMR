# Leafjourney Marketplace — Product Requirements Document

> Classification: Implementation-grade specification
> Source: Scott Wayman (CPTO), April 2026
> Status: Active build

## Product Thesis

Leafjourney Marketplace transforms cannabis commerce from a generic
dispensary transaction into a premium, physician-curated, clinically
informed patient experience. By integrating commerce directly into the
EMR ecosystem, Leafjourney creates a new category: care-aware
e-commerce for a cash-pay wellness market.

**We are not building a dispensary storefront. We are building the most
trusted commerce layer in cannabis care.**

The marketplace is the monetization surface, the retention surface,
and the patient trust surface of the Leafjourney platform.

## Strategic Differentiator

The moat is commerce fused with care intelligence. Because the
marketplace sits inside a broader EMR ecosystem, it can know:

- Who the patient is
- What symptoms they have
- What products they have used before
- What a clinician recommended
- What their care plan includes
- What worked previously
- What formats and cannabinoid profiles they prefer
- When they are due for a refill
- What may not be appropriate due to interactions

That is the moat. Not prettier product cards.

## Design Direction

The design should feel like:
- Apple Store meets premium telehealth
- Modern wellness brand meets luxury minimalism
- Elite Shopify execution meets clinical intelligence

Not like:
- A crowded dispensary menu
- A loud DTC gimmick brand
- A generic template with weed leaves and a gradient having an
  identity crisis

## MVP Scope

1. Premium storefront landing with hero + featured products
2. Search with autosuggest
3. Category navigation (by symptom, format, goal, cannabinoid)
4. Polished product grid with trust badges
5. Product detail with cannabinoid profile + clinician commentary
6. Clinician Picks collection
7. Trust strip (physician-curated, lab-tested, secure)
8. Cart + checkout flow
9. Mobile-first responsive experience
10. Symptom/goal-based browsing entry points
